Visual Clutter is a public code repository where I am starting to collect various vision side projects. These will include vision algorithm demos and optical illusions.

Non-Contact Pulse Monitoring: course project for graduate computer vision. I attempted to measure human heart rate by blind source separation on standard video recordings–the results were poor but I am still working to improve this project. This is a re-implementation of the system described in the paper Non-contact, automated cardiac pulse measurements using video imaging and blind source separation by Poh, McDuff, and Picard published in Optics Express (2010).

Prokudin-Gorsky Plate Registration: Prokudin-Gorsky convinced the Tsar to send him across Russia to document the country by color photography in 1909. No real means to record or view color photos existed at the time, so he captured each image by taking separate photos with different filters. This project creates color photos by automatic registration and color channel assignment of the original plates. This is done by maximization of mutual information and evaluating KL-divergence of color distributions. (Full write-up coming soon.)

These color photographs were created automatically by registering separate color plates and assigning each plate to the appropriate color. The misaligned result on the right illustrates the problem of excessive binning: estimation of the color distribution has too little evidence to be accurate.

Congealing Digits: congealing is a computer vision method for the joint alignment of images developed by Professor Learned-Miller. I implemented the algorithm and demonstrated the alignment of images of handwritten 3’s and 5’s from the MNIST digits data set. (Full write-up coming soon.)

Mean images produced by averaging a set of handwritten 3s together before and after congealing. The aligned result is much more coherent.

Until I finish full write-ups of these two projects, you can take a look at the Prokudin-Gorsky registration README and the Congealing README.
